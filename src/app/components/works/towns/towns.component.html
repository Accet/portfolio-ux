{{ 'Overview' | uppercase }}

1000 Towns of Canada website is to help a user to find exciting and unique small towns all over Canada that they have never heard of before. Small towns in their turn will benefit from this project as well. It can stimulate their local economy and bring more money into municipal budgets thank to numerous travelers who might become return customers of local businesses.

{{ 'research' | uppercase }}

Our research began with brainstorming who needs to be interviewed and who needs to take part in our surveys. Every single individual who can contribute to creating our problem-solving design is precious to us. We chose 3 groups of the target audience for our surveys and interviews: travelers, small-town citizens, and officials.

{{ 'persona' | uppercase }}

We summarized information through our research and created two personas. We will then base our future UX designs for the primary needs of these two users.

{{ 'IDENTIFYING PROBLEM' | uppercase }}

Tourists are not even aware of the fact that there are plenty of exciting little towns around them and that is why they find it difficult to choose one to visit. Finding accurate information online is another challenge.

{{ 'IDENTIFYING SOLUTION' | uppercase }}

Design a website to increase awareness of the small towns of Canada, as well as to help travellers plan their trips to towns.

{{ 'DESIGN ' | uppercase }}

Traveller’s Pain Point:

Tourists are not even aware about exciting little towns around them.

Community’s Pain Point:

Some small towns are not presented on travelling resources to visit.

Design Decision:

From Landing Page a traveller can easily find interested town. Ambassadors can add their town with one click.

Traveller’s Pain Point:

Travellers can set distance from their home town and they can see all small town around.

Design Decisions:

  • Distance filter helps tourists to find a town around;
  • Activity filters filter search result according to tourist interests;

Traveller’s Pain Point:

Travellers can see all important information about town on one page, as well as events and things to do.

Community’s Pain Point:

Design Decisions:

Ambassador can see all important information that can attract tourists in one page.
