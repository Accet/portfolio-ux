{{ 'Overview' | uppercase }}

Binqit is an online job market platform focused on helping students find part-time jobs fast. Moreover, what so many young people love about it is that there is no need to create a resume. In addition, the app's vetting algorithm will match students with best-fit jobs based on their skills, experience, and hourly availability.

{{ 'research' | uppercase }}

Our research objective was to find out what is crucial for students when they search for jobs and what is important for employers when they look for potential student employees. 4 business owners and 11 job seekers were interviewed, 47 survey responses were analyzed.

{{ 'persona' | uppercase }}

We summarized information through our research and created two personas. Later on, we specifically based on our UX designs on these users' primary needs.

{{ 'IDENTIFYING PROBLEM' | uppercase }}

{{ 'IDENTIFYING SOLUTION' | uppercase }}

Currently there is no platform specifically designed for a part-time job search, which could make this process quick, simple, convenient and transparent.

{{ 'DESIGN ' | uppercase }}

Job Seeker’s Pain Point:

Regular applications lack availability scheduling function.

Employer’s Pain Point:

Same as job seeker's - regular applications lack availability scheduling function.

Design Decision:

Input availability scheduling function.

Job Seeker’s Pain Point:

Job search is time-consuming;

Design Decisions:

  • Every Job post shows clear and precise information defining whether a job seeker fits for the job;
  • Responsibilities as bullet points;
  • Requirements as tags;
  • Specific hours required;
  • One click apply.

Job Seeker’s Pain Point:

No feedback from businesses they have applied to and been declined by because business owners are busy and forgetful.

Design Decisions:

Automated decline messaging.
