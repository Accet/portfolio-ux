{{ 'Overview' | uppercase }}

Binqit is an online job market platform focused on helping students find part-time jobs fast, moreover what so many young people love about it is that there is no need to create a resume. In addition, the app's vetting algorithm will match students with best-fit jobs based on their skills, experience, and hourly availability.

{{ 'research' | uppercase }}

Our research objective was to find out what is crucial for students when they search for jobs and what is important for employers when they look for potential student employees. 4 business owners and 11 job seekers were interviewed, 47 survey responses.

{{ 'persona' | uppercase }}

We summarized information through our research and created two personas. We will then base our future UX designs for the primary needs of these two users.s

{{ 'IDENTIFYING PROBLEM' | uppercase }}

There is no platform specifically for part-time job searching that makes the process quick, easy, convenient, and transparent.

{{ 'IDENTIFYING SOLUTION' | uppercase }}

Create a platform that matches job seekers with jobs, has built-in messaging and scheduling, and provides timely application status updates.

{{ 'DESIGN ' | uppercase }}

Job Seeker’s Pain Point:

Regular applications lack availability scheduling function.

Employer’s Pain Point:

Same as job seekers - regular applications lack availability scheduling function.

Design Decision:

Input availability scheduling function.

Job Seeker’s Pain Point:

Job search is time consuming;

Design Decisions:

  • Every Job post shows clear and precise information defining whether a job seeker fits for the job;
  • Responsibilities as bullet points;
  • Requirements as tags;
  • Specific hours required;
  • 1 click apply.

Job Seeker’s Pain Point:

No feedback from businesses they have applied to and been declined from because business owners are busy and forgetful.

Design Decisions:

Automated decline messaging.
